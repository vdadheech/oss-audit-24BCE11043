Every day, I rely on Linux — a piece of software
that someone built, shared, and refused to lock away.
To me, freedom means Choice.
That single word is why the Linux Kernel exists.

In 1991, Linus Torvalds did not have to share his
work. He chose to — and in doing so, he gave the
world a foundation that powers billions of devices.

I believe in that same spirit. One day I want to
build an Operating system — and I will share it freely, the way
the kernel was shared with the world.
Because open source is not just a licence.
It is a promise: knowledge belongs to everyone.
